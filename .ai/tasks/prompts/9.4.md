# Task Prompt: 9.4 - Download and transfer feedback redesign

## Objective

Move the human-readable `launchpad download` flow and its shared transfer
feedback onto the Phase 9 restrained visual grammar while preserving existing
download behavior, confirmation semantics, and machine-readable output.

## Context

- The active coordination branch is `phase/09-cli-visual-overhaul`.
- Tasks `9.1` through `9.3` are merged. The shared display primitives, updated
  key/value contrast, and the new submit/status layout are already on the
  phase branch.
- `download` still uses older summary-table and panel-oriented confirmation
  patterns in `src/launchpad_cli/cli/download.py`, even though the Phase 9
  design brief calls for a clean preflight summary, minimal transfer feedback,
  and a restrained completion surface.
- The design brief section
  `.ai/plans/LAUNCHPAD_UI_REDESIGN_PLAN.md` under
  `3.5 launchpad download — Results Download` is the detailed reference for
  this task's pre-download summary, progress feedback, and post-download
  verification output.
- Keep the stronger key/value label contrast introduced during `9.2`/`9.3`;
  labels should remain readable navigational text, not dim footnotes.

## In Scope

- Redesign the human-readable pre-download summary and confirmation flow for
  `launchpad download`, including the remote/local size, compression estimate,
  and destination details already available in the current plan.
- Replace panel-heavy or ad hoc download success output with the restrained
  Phase 9 completion layout and lightweight next steps.
- Bring shared transfer progress/spinner feedback used by the download flow
  onto the minimal Phase 9 style without changing transfer execution logic.
- Keep existing flags, cleanup semantics, task selection behavior, and JSON
  payloads unchanged.
- Update or add regression coverage for the redesigned human-readable download
  and transfer feedback surfaces.

## Out of Scope

- Redesigning `logs`, `ls`, `cancel`, or `cleanup`
- Changing download planning, archive creation, transfer strategy selection,
  checksum behavior, or cleanup logic beyond presentation-layer adjustments
- Changing `--json`, `--yes`, `--quiet`, `--no-color`, or selector semantics
- Docs/README refresh beyond inline comments or helper notes needed to support
  the implementation

## Deliverables

- Phase 9 human-readable download preflight and completion output
- Phase 9 minimal progress/spinner feedback for the download flow
- Regression coverage for the updated download and transfer feedback surfaces
