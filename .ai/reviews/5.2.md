# Review: 5.2

**Verdict:** REVISION_NEEDED

## Summary
The new transfer-mode surface, worker-pool paths, and test coverage are in
place, but the current striped upload assembly logic undermines the Phase 5
design by proxying the entire payload back through the local client during
remote assembly. That turns a single upload into an upload-plus-download-plus-
upload cycle, which defeats the intended fast-path for large payloads.

## Issues
1. [`src/launchpad_cli/core/transfer.py`](/Users/srogachev/Projects/NEW/Launchpad-CLI/src/launchpad_cli/core/transfer.py#L588) assembles uploaded parts by reading each remote part over SFTP and writing it back into the final remote file through the same client connection. Because the bytes traverse the workstation again, `submit --transfer-mode single-file` effectively retransfers the entire archive during assembly instead of performing a true remote-side concatenate step. This is a major performance regression against the accepted Phase 5 design and likely worse than the old single-stream upload for large payloads.

## Required Changes
1. Replace the client-mediated `_assemble_remote_parts()` flow with a true remote-side assembly step on the cluster host, such as a shell-command concatenate into `<archive>.partial` followed by size verification and atomic rename. Keep the same deterministic staging directory and cleanup behavior, but do not stream the uploaded parts back through the local machine during assembly.
