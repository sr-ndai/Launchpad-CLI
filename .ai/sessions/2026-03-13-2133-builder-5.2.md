## What Was Done

- Added the Phase 5 public transfer surface for submit and download:
  `--transfer-mode` with `auto`, `single-file`, and `multi-file`, plus
  config-driven `--streams`.
- Implemented striped single-file transfer helpers in
  `src/launchpad_cli/core/transfer.py` using deterministic temporary parts,
  safe remote/local assembly, resume-by-part behavior, and stream fallback.
- Implemented worker-pool many-file transfer helpers in
  `src/launchpad_cli/core/transfer.py` and wired them into submit/download.
- Updated submit to support archive-backed `single-file` transfer and direct
  `multi-file` transfer when `--no-compress` is explicit.
- Updated download to replace the old archive/raw surface with the new public
  transfer modes while keeping `--remote-compress` as a deprecated alias.
- Expanded tests to cover striped assembly, many-file worker pools, CLI
  wiring, and new mode/stream behavior.

## Observations

- Conservative interpretation: `submit --transfer-mode multi-file` is only
  valid when `--no-compress` is explicit, so the command does not silently
  change archive semantics under the operator.
- The new stream fallback reduces effective concurrency only when the failure
  looks like a server-side session/channel/SFTP ceiling; normal transfer and
  integrity failures still surface immediately.
